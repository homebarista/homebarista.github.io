Why Does Coffee Need Degassing? It's Better To Drink After Degassing

Many beginners of coffee have heard that degassing needs to be stored in one-way exhaust valve bags or one-way exhaust valve cans. Indeed, these steps will make the flavor of coffee beans better because various factors and the characteristics of coffee beans produced in different countries and estates make the degassing method and time difference. This article will take you to understand what degassing is.

Carbon Dioxide: Coffee Bean Respiration

Take pour over coffee as an example. When hot water is added for steaming, it will release a large amount of carbon dioxide, which makes it feel like coffee beans are "breathing". This is also the standard for many people to judge whether a cup of pour over coffee is "fresh enough"; Espresso crema is also covered with carbon dioxide by thin foam, so there will be a beautiful layer of klinma crema when coffee beans are fresh enough are brewed. When you drink espresso, you can observe the freshness of the coffee brewed by the store. Crimson and ochre crema represent that the coffee is still very fresh, while golden yellow is beautiful but not so fresh.

As a coffee fan, you need to understand how temperature affects the flavor and aroma of your coffee.

The fresher the coffee, the more carbon dioxide it contains. But if you have to go to a coffee shop to replenish it in two or three days, it may not be practical and troublesome. Coffee beans are very noble products and easily affect humidity, oxygen, light source, or heat temperature. The best way to preserve them is in a cool and dry place, and it is the most difficult to eliminate the air. Moreover, the air in Taiwan is humid.

One way ventilation valve - a good partner for prolonging the preservation and degassing of coffee

It would be great if we could keep the air out!

Yes, the one-way exhaust valve achieves this effect. The coffee bean bag / can with the one-way exhaust valve will sink the carbon dioxide released by the coffee beans and then squeeze the lighter oxygen and nitrogen out of the bag/can. Just like the cut apple will turn yellow when it touches oxygen, and the coffee bean will start qualitative change when it touches oxygen. In order to prevent these qualitative change factors, it is the right choice to use the packaging with a one-way exhaust valve. Degassing Time

At the beginning of learning, some coffee players thought that the coffee beans roasted yesterday could get the freshest flavor by brewing and drinking immediately today. When brewing, they found that the taste was not as good as expected and even doubted whether there was a problem with their technology. They didn't find it getting better until they tried for two weeks, but those coffee beans also saw the bottom. This means that they didn't degass the coffee beans, but they couldn't brew them to the best state. This is a small mistake that many novices will make, but how can you know what is a better flavor without experiencing mistakes and trying?

Coffee beans are like living people. People oxidize cells with the inhalation of oxygen, which is also a process of aging. The freshly roasted coffee is too young and has not reached the most mature stage. Like people, you will become more mature after years of growth. What you need is to give coffee beans some time.

General Degassing Time: How To Distinguish According To Roasting Degree

In fact, there is no specific schedule for degassing, which can only be based on experience or the advice given by the roaster.

The degassing time of very shallow roasting is recommended to be 14 days; Lightly roasting in 10 days; Medium roasted for seven days; Medium-dark roasting takes 4 to 5 days, and the general recommended degassing time is as above. Because in the pour over single product coffee, the coffee can be enough to wake up when steaming. If it is Espresso beans, the degassing time is generally 21 days, but the coffee beans used for business do not have so much time to grow, so it will accelerate the degassing under contact with the air so that the coffee beans can reach the smooth stage. If brewed too early, it may cause discomfort and throat blockage.
